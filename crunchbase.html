Growth Insight

updated Mar 9, 2025 Not Growing

Tesla reported its first annual decline in vehicle deliveries in 2024, with total deliveries of 1.79 million vehicles, down from 1.81 million in 2023, marking a 1.1% decrease. This decline was attributed to increased competition and a shift in consumer preferences. Additionally, Tesla's sales in Europe fell by 45% in January 2025 compared to the previous year, with only 9,945 vehicles sold, despite a 37% increase in overall EV sales in the region.

The company also faced challenges in China, where sales of China-made electric vehicles dropped by 49.2% in February 2025. Despite these declines, Tesla's energy storage deployments reached a record 31.4 GWh in 2024, a 114% increase from 14.7 GWh in 2023, indicating growth in its energy segment. Tesla's stock price has fluctuated significantly, dropping below $1 trillion in market capitalization due to these sales challenges and public backlash against CEO Elon Musk's political involvement.
